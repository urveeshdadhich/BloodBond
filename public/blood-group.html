Know Your Blood Group

Learn about different blood types and how to get tested

What are Blood Groups?

Blood groups are determined by the presence or absence of certain antigens on the surface of red blood cells. The ABO system classifies blood into four groups: A, B, AB, and O. Additionally, the Rhesus (Rh) factor determines if your blood is positive (+) or negative (-).

The 8 Main Blood Types:

  • A+ - 35.7% of population
  • A- - 6.3% of population
  • B+ - 8.5% of population
  • B- - 1.5% of population
  • AB+ - 3.4% of population
  • AB- - 0.6% of population
  • O+ - 37.4% of population
  • O- - 6.6% of population

How to Get Tested

There are several ways to find out your blood type:

Blood Donation

When you donate blood, the donation center will typically test your blood type and inform you.

Doctor's Office

Your doctor can order a blood type test as part of routine lab work.

At-Home Test Kits

You can purchase at-home blood typing kits from pharmacies or online retailers.
